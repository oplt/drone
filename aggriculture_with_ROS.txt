  How it works
  Stage 4:

  - ROS 2 capture_sync_node subscribes to /camera/image_raw and /drone/state
  - It nearest-timestamp matches image frames to state samples, enforces IRRIGATION_CAPTURE_INTERVAL_S, writes a mission-scoped spool file, then uploads the frame and geotag metadata to POST /irrigation/captures

  Stage 5:

  - The backend compositor places images using lat/lon + altitude + assumed FOV and emits a stitched preview overlay plus capture footprints

  Stage 6:

  - The analytics engine computes RGB vegetation/stress proxies and local deviation scores
  - It emits under_irrigated, overwatered, and uneven_distribution zones with severity, confidence, polygons, and ranked inspection points

  Stage 7:

  - FieldPage polls /irrigation/missions/{mission_id}/summary
  - The dashboard now shows processing state, capture count, stitched overlay, anomaly polygons, and inspection markers

  MVP limitations

  - The stitched layer is an approximate geotag/FOV composite, not a survey-grade orthomosaic
  - Analytics are RGB heuristics, not agronomic ground truth
  - Uneven-band detection is spatial/elongation based, not crop-row semantic analysis
  - ROS 2 state/output messages use JSON on std_msgs/String to avoid introducing custom message packages in the MVP
  - The bridge assumes a ROS 2 image topic exists or is republished; it does not auto-discover Gazebo camera topics
